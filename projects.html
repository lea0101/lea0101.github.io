Project: Cuban Missile Crisis Simulation

As part of a course on the Cold War, I made this game to simulate the Cuban Missile Crisis. It focuses on the game theory aspect of this crisis, where the decisions made by each player (the US and the USSR) have varying chances of producing the desired outcome.

"Winning the game" boils down to the same principle that has been cited when we've brought up threats of nuclear war—the only winning move is not to play. In this game, you play against a USSR that chooses its actions randomly, with no way of predicting what may happen. Consequently, you'll find that it is quite difficult to get out of such a situation without ending up at mutually assured destruction.

This project was made in Godot, with art created in GIMP, both of which are free and open-source applications. Other informative sources are cited within the game.

Project: WiCyS CTF
